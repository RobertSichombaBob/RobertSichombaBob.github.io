Field Density Verification of AC-16C Modified Asphalt Surface Course: Core Sample Testing and Quality Assurance

Robert Sichomba, PhD

Materials Engineer | Geotechnical Specialist

Published: October 15, 2025

Reading time: 20 minutes

Executive Summary

This technical report presents the results of field density verification testing conducted on AC-16C modified asphalt surface course at chainage SBCH96+060 to SBCH96+740 on the Lusaka to Ndola Road Project in Zambia. Core samples were extracted and tested according to JTG E20-2011 standards to determine in-situ density and compaction degree. The average compaction achieved was 95.1%, exceeding the specified minimum requirement of 93.0%. These results demonstrate exceptional quality control and construction practices, ensuring long-term pavement performance and durability.

Key Performance Indicators

1. Introduction

The compaction degree of asphalt surface courses is a critical quality control parameter that directly influences pavement performance, durability, and resistance to traffic loading. Proper compaction ensures optimal binder distribution, minimizes air voids, and provides the structural integrity required for heavy-duty applications.

This report details the field density verification testing conducted on AC-16C modified asphalt surface course at chainage SBCH96+060 to SBCH96+740 on the Lusaka to Ndola Road Project. Core samples were extracted and analyzed according to JTG E20-2011 "Standard Test Methods of Bitumen and Bituminous Mixtures for Highway Engineering" to verify compliance with project specifications.

Figure 1: Core sample extraction from AC-16C modified asphalt surface course at SBCH96+620.

2. Testing Methodology and Standards

The testing program was conducted in accordance with the following standards and specifications:

Applicable Standards

  • JTG E20-2011: Standard Test Methods of Bitumen and Bituminous Mixtures for Highway Engineering
  • Project Technical Specifications: Minimum compaction degree of 93.0% for AC-16C surface course
  • Maximum Theoretical Relative Density: Determined as 2.813 g/cm³

2.1 Core Sample Testing Procedure

Core Sample Testing Protocol:

  1. Sample Extraction: Core samples (100mm diameter) extracted using diamond coring equipment at specified locations
  2. Sample Preparation: Samples cleaned, trimmed to uniform thickness, and conditioned at 25°C for 24 hours
  3. Mass Measurements: Three mass measurements taken:
    • Mass in air (Ma)
    • Mass in water (Mw)
    • Surface-dry mass (Ms)
  4. Bulk Density Calculation: Using hydrostatic weighing method
  5. Compaction Degree: Calculated as ratio of bulk density to maximum theoretical density

3. Mathematical Formulations and Calculations

The following mathematical formulations were used to determine bulk density and compaction degree:

3.1 Bulk Relative Density Calculation

$$\rho_b = \frac{M_a}{M_s - M_w}$$

Where:
\(\rho_b\) = Bulk relative density
\(M_a\) = Mass of sample in air (g)
\(M_s\) = Surface-dry mass of sample (g)
\(M_w\) = Mass of sample in water (g)

Alternative Formula (when surface-dry mass not available):

$$\rho_b = \frac{M_a}{M_a - M_w} \times \rho_w$$

Where:
\(\rho_w\) = Density of water (g/cm³)

3.2 Compaction Degree Calculation

$$\text{Compaction Degree (\%)} = \frac{\rho_b}{\rho_t} \times 100\%$$

Where:
\(\rho_t\) = Maximum theoretical relative density (2.813 g/cm³)

Example Calculation (Sample 1):

\(\rho_b = \frac{1062.3}{1066.8 - 667.8} = \frac{1062.3}{399.0} = 2.662 \, \text{g/cm}^3\)

\(\text{Compaction Degree} = \frac{2.662}{2.813} \times 100\% = 94.7\%\)

4.1 Quality Assessment

Quality Assessment Summary:

Excellent Performance: All core samples exceeded the minimum compaction requirement of 93.0%, with an average compaction degree of 95.1%. This represents a 2.1% margin above specification, indicating exceptional quality control during construction.

Uniform Compaction: The consistent compaction values across different locations (94.7% to 95.5%) demonstrate uniform compaction throughout the tested section, ensuring consistent pavement performance.

Structural Integrity: The achieved compaction levels ensure optimal air void content (approximately 4.9%), providing excellent resistance to moisture damage, rutting, and fatigue cracking.

Figure 2: Laboratory testing of asphalt core samples using hydrostatic weighing method.

5. Engineering Significance of Test Results

The test results have significant implications for pavement performance and structural design:

5.1 Pavement Performance Prediction

The achieved compaction degree of 95.1% corresponds to an air void content of approximately 4.9%, which is within the optimal range of 3-5% for surface courses. This ensures excellent resistance to moisture damage, oxidation, and thermal cracking while providing adequate skid resistance.

5.2 Structural Capacity Enhancement

Each 1% increase in compaction above the minimum requirement can increase the structural number (SN) by approximately 0.05-0.10. The 2.1% margin achieved provides enhanced structural capacity, extending pavement life by an estimated 15-20% compared to minimum specification compliance.

5.3 Quality Assurance and Risk Mitigation

The consistent compaction results across multiple locations demonstrate robust quality control procedures and skilled construction practices. This reduces the risk of premature pavement distress and minimizes long-term maintenance requirements.

6. Quality Control Recommendations

Based on the excellent test results, the following recommendations are provided:

Quality Control Protocol:

  1. Maintain Current Practices: Continue with the existing compaction procedures and quality control measures.
  2. Regular Monitoring: Conduct core sampling at minimum 1 test per 1000m² of laid surface course.
  3. Temperature Control: Ensure asphalt placement temperature remains within specified range (150-165°C for modified asphalt).
  4. Rolling Pattern: Maintain the current rolling pattern with appropriate roller types and passes.
  5. Documentation: Continue detailed record-keeping of all test results for quality assurance and payment verification.

Project Recommendation:

The AC-16C modified asphalt surface course at SBCH96+060 to SBCH96+740 demonstrates exceptional quality with an average compaction degree of 95.1%, exceeding the specified requirement of 93.0%. The construction team should be commended for their excellent workmanship and quality control practices. Continue with the current construction methodology for subsequent sections.

7. Conclusion

The field density verification testing of AC-16C modified asphalt surface course at SBCH96+060 to SBCH96+740 demonstrates exceptional construction quality with an average compaction degree of 95.1%, exceeding the specified minimum requirement of 93.0% by a significant margin.

The consistent compaction results across multiple locations indicate uniform quality throughout the tested section, ensuring optimal pavement performance, durability, and structural integrity. The achieved compaction levels provide excellent resistance to traffic loading, environmental exposure, and moisture damage.

These results reflect the high standards of quality control and construction practices employed on the Lusaka to Ndola Road Project, positioning it as a benchmark for infrastructure development in the region. The demonstrated commitment to quality assurance ensures long-term pavement performance and minimizes lifecycle maintenance costs.
