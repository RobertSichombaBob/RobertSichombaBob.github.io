Technical Characterization of AC-16C Coarse Aggregate

Lusaka–Ndola Dual Carriageway Project | Zambia
Source: K112 Quarry Material: AC-16C Modified Asphalt Mixture Standards: JTG 3432-2024 CML
December 2024 8 min read 1,800 words

1. Introduction

Coarse aggregate forms the load-bearing skeleton of asphalt concrete. For AC-16C, it must provide:

  • High interlock via cubical shape and angular particles
  • Resistance to crushing and abrasion under heavy traffic loading
  • Strong adhesion with polymer-modified bitumen (PG76-10)

This comprehensive evaluation of material from K112 Quarry follows JTG 3432-2024 and CML protocols, ensuring compliance with international standards for high-performance pavement construction.

3. Mechanical Performance Characterization

3.1 Aggregate Crushing Value (ACV)

Measures resistance to compressive fracture under traffic loading conditions:

$$\text{ACV} = \frac{m_{\text{fines}}}{m_{\text{total}}} \times 100\%$$

Where $m_{\text{fines}}$ represents material passing 2.36 mm after application of 400 kN load.

Test Condition Specification Limit Result (%) Engineering Assessment
Normal Temperature≤20%13.6✅ Excellent
Elevated Temperature≤24%14.7✅ Superior

3.2 Los Angeles Abrasion (LAA)

Quantifies wear resistance from aggregate–aggregate and aggregate–tire interaction:

$$\text{LAA} = \frac{m_1 - m_2}{m_1} \times 100\%$$

Experimental parameters: $m_1 = 5003 \text{ g}$, $m_2 = 3970.5 \text{ g}$

$$\text{LAA} = \frac{5003 - 3970.5}{5003} \times 100\% = 20.6\%$$

Average LAA = 20.4% → Significantly below 28% specification limit

3.3 Flakiness Index (FI)

  • Adhesion Grade: 5/5 (no peeling observed)

5. Engineering Performance Predictions

5.1 Rutting Resistance Analysis

Low ACV (13.6%) and LAA (20.4%) values ensure minimal permanent deformation under ESALs exceeding 30 million.

5.2 Fatigue Performance

Cubical particle geometry (FI < 8%) and strong mechanical interlock significantly reduce microcrack propagation under repeated loading cycles.

5.3 Moisture Damage Resistance

Grade 5 adhesion characteristics combined with low absorption and clean surface morphology provide exceptional resistance to stripping phenomena.

7. Technical Conclusion

The comprehensive characterization demonstrates that coarse aggregate from K112 Quarry exhibits:

  • Mechanical robustness with exceptional resistance to crushing and abrasion
  • Geometric optimality through cubical particle shape and optimal gradation
  • Chemical compatibility with SBS-modified bitumen systems
  • Exceptional cleanliness and long-term durability characteristics

This material fully satisfies all technical requirements for high-performance AC-16C modified asphalt concrete in the Lusaka–Ndola Dual Carriageway project, ensuring pavement service life exceeding design specifications.
